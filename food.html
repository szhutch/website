FOOD

I used to be a fussy eater as a child but thankfully have grown out of that. Now I love food; eating and making it. During Sixth Form at school I took a cookery course the Leith's Intermediate Course in Food and Wine. This opened me to experiencing and cooking many interesting and tasty foods. When restaurants were open I enjoyed sampling all sorts of cuisines. During lockdown I have been experimenting with bread making and have made sourdough, focaccia and pitta bread.

KOOKOO SABZI

This dish is from the cookbook: From a Persian Kitchen by Atoosa Sepehr. Kookoo Sabzi or known in English as a fresh herb fritatta with feta cheese is a dish that was originally eaten for Persian New Year, although can be eaten all year round.

FOCACCIA

Originating from Italy, Focaccia is for me one of the easiest breads to make. Using yeast for a quick rise. This bread is very tasty and can be made with a combination of toppings/ ingredients. My favourite is the classic rosemary focaccia.

SOURDOUGH

Many people have become Sourdough fanatics during lockdown - if they can find flour! I had made sourdough before lockdown but created a new starter as I had thrown away my previous one when moving. The starter improves with age.

SPINACH, CHICKPEA & LENTIL CURRY

This curry is a delicious and heartwarming vegetarian/ vegan meal. There are many variaitons of curreh to be made from some basic ingredients, many of which are tinned which is useful if you want to limit your trips to the supermarket. Although it is nice to use some fresh veg too like spinach!
